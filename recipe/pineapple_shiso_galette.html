Pineapple Shiso Galette

deliciously bright

Ingredients & Steps

one 14" round galette / two 10" rounds

sugared shiso

( Reserve leftover egg yolk and egg white for egg wash )

Brush shiso with a thin layer of egg white on both sides


Pat shiso into sour sugar mixture

Transfer to wire rack

Dry for 24 hours

galette dough

Using a bench knife, chop butter into flour


Toss coconut oil and remaining butter into the flour


Add water 1 tbsp at a time until dough begins to clump

Gently knead/press the dough and separate hydrated dough onto saran wrap

Hydrate the remaining flour and add to the dough pile

Wrap the dough and flatten into a square

Refrigerate for at least 2 hours

galette assembly

Remove from fridge and rest at room temperature for 5 minutes

Prepare parchment paper

Laminate dough

Shape into a 14+" round


Massage lime zest into sugar

Evenly sprinkle over dough

Fold edges of the dough

Chill in fridge momentarily

Preheat oven to 425°


( Save pineapple scraps for glaze )

Cut the pineapple into six sections

Cut 1/4" slices

Arrange and overlap pineapple onto the crust


Finish the edges with egg wash and sugar

Bake for 40 minutes, until the entire crust is deeply golden with dark spots

Cool for 5 minutes

( While galette is baking, make the glaze )

glaze

Bring to a boil

Simmer on medium-high heat for 20 minutes

Filter syrup

Reduce on low-medium heat until sufficiently thick


Stir to combine

Transfer baked galette to wire rack and remove parchment paper

Brush a thin but thorough layer of glaze over the pineapple

final touches

Right before serving, arrange sugared shiso
