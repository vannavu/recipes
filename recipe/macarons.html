Macarons

tedious scrumptious

Ingredients & Steps

30 cookies

base

Prepare piping bags and line two cookie sheets with parchment paper

Sift powdered sugar and almond flour into egg whites

Mix until egg white is roughly incorporated

syrup

Heat over medium-high heat, do not stir

Keep an eye on the temperature once it boils

Meanwhile…

meringue

Stand mixer with whisk attachment : once the syrup starts boiling, whip the egg whites on speed 4

When the eggs whites foam, add the granulated sugar

When the meringue has reached soft peaks and the syrup has reached 240°, slowly pour the syrup into the meringue at the lowest speed

Increase to speed 8 and whip to stiff peaks

batter

Fold 1/3 of meringue into base

Fold the rest of the meringue in until drops of batter meld back into the bowl

Fill piping bags

Pipe 1" circles

Tap cookie sheets to pop air bubbles

Rest cookies until a skin forms (10 minutes)

300° : 10–15 minutes (until shells stop jiggling)

Cool completely before filling

coconut lime french buttercream


Heat coconut milk until simmering

Pull off heat and add yolk mixture

Place back on heat and stir for 1 minute

Strain custard through fine sieve into stand mixer bowl

Mix on speed 6 until the bowl is no longer warm to the touch


Lower to speed 2 and add butter in chunks

Increase to speed 6 until mixture lightens and is a thick cream consistency


Use immediately

Sources

Notes

  • Macarons taste best resting in the fridge for 24 hours
  • Do not make on a humid day (meringue will not whip)
